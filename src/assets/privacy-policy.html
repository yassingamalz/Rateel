This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our Rateel mobile application (the "App"). Please read this Privacy Policy carefully. By using the App, you consent to the practices described in this policy. ## Information We Collect ### Information You Provide to Us - **Audio Recordings**: When you use the pronunciation practice features, we temporarily process audio recordings of your voice. - **User Progress Data**: We collect information about your progress through lessons, quiz scores, and completion status. - **Device Permissions**: We request microphone access to enable the pronunciation practice features. ### Information Automatically Collected - **Usage Data**: Information about how you use the App, including interaction with features and time spent on lessons. - **Device Information**: Type of mobile device, operating system version, and device identifiers. - **Performance Data**: App performance metrics and crash reports to improve user experience. ## How We Use Your Information We use the information we collect to: - Provide, maintain, and improve the App functionality - Track your progress through lessons and provide appropriate content - Analyze usage patterns to enhance user experience - Fix bugs and troubleshoot technical issues - Develop new features based on user needs ## Data Storage and Security - **Local Storage**: Most of your data, including progress information and practice recordings, is stored locally on your device. - **No Cloud Storage**: We do not upload or store your audio recordings on our servers. - **Security Measures**: We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect your data. ## Data Sharing and Disclosure We do not sell, trade, or otherwise transfer your information to third parties. We may share information in the following limited circumstances: - With your consent - To comply with legal obligations - To protect our rights, privacy, safety, or property ## Children's Privacy The App is not directed to children under the age of 13.
